2 firmware of the dedicated motion processor.

4 ## Goals
5 
6 The motion processor is required to :
7 * keep track of the current absolute position and heading of the robot
8 * provide an high level interface to accurately control the motion of the robot
9 
10 The robot should be able to go to any goal position and heading using a trajectory composed of two arcs and a straight line.
11 Here is an example :
12 
13 ![trajectory](specs/trajectory.jpeg)
14 
15 Because the robot is not capable of infinite linear nor angular acceleration, maximum accelerations should be defined and
16 the speed profiles should look like (for example) :
17 
18 ![speeds](specs/speeds.png)
19 
20 Given the intricate relation between motion and high level considerations, the computation of the trajectory won't be performed by the
21 motion coprocessor. It will receive position control commands :
22 * maximum linear and angular accelerations
23 * linear and angular cruise speeds
24 * goal mean distance (mean distance being the mean between the distance of the two wheels)
25 * goal heading, and eventually a reference mean distance to start the rotation. This should allow for a precise synchronization between
26 translation and rotation.
27 
28 This way of controlling motion provide a flexible interface without overloading the I2C bus through which the commands are transmitted,
29 nor requiring any real time capabilities from the master.
30 
31 The MotionController will provide a **position control**, so that the robot is either moving toward its goal or
32 holding its current position.
33 
34 The real position should also be tracked, using a fusion of the data from the encoder wheels, the IMU and radio measurements.
35 A special attention should be given to the IMU response time. Indeed when rotating, it is suspected that the heading measurement lags.
36 
37 ## Interface
38 
39 The MotionController should provide read/write data to the I2C master mapped as 8 or 16 bits registers :
40 
41 * current x absolute position (read)
42 * current y absolute position (read)
43 * current heading (read/write)
44 
45 <br>
46 
47 * current right wheel distance (read/write)
48 * current left wheel distance (read/write)
49 
50 <br>
51 
52 * maximum accelaration (read/write)
53 * maximum angular accelaration (read/write)
54 * cruise speed (read/write)
55 * cruise angular speed (read/write)
56 * goal mean distance (read/write)
57 * goal heading (read/write)
58 * heading distance sync reference (read/write)<br>
59 
60 <br>
61 
62 * linear PID coefficients (read/write, flash stored)
63 * angular PID coefficients (read/write, flash stored)
64 
65 Any distance is in mm.
66 Angle range is [0, 360].
67 
68 ## "Registers" address and size
69 
70 The motorboard listens on address 0x12 (device address).
71 
72 Configuration values are placed first, then data and finally targets.
73 All write-only values are read-as-zero (RAZ).
74 Writing to a read-only value is implementation-defined, it will defined later.
75 For 32 bits value, split into 2 16-bit register, the LOW register must always be
76 read/written first. Failing to follow this rule will lead to invalid data.
